# 02 — Consulting marketing playbook

> The boutique-consultancy money equation:
> **(wedge) × (named method) × (visible proof) × (distribution) × (premium pricing) × (senior-only delivery)**
>
> Miss any factor → revenue tends to zero. Compound them → inbound starts and you stop pitching.

## The canon

### Tier 1 — the bibles

**David Maister — *Managing the Professional Service Firm* (1993); *True Professionalism* (1997); *The Trusted Advisor* (2000, with Charles Green & Robert Galford).**
30+ years old, still the foundation. Read *Trusted Advisor* first — it gives you the **trust equation**:
> Trust = (Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy) / Self-Orientation
DS2's challenge-first method is a *machine for raising the numerator (intimacy + credibility) while lowering self-orientation*. Maister wrote the playbook DS2 is already half-living.

**Blair Enns — *The Win Without Pitching Manifesto* (2010); *The Four Conversations* (2023); *Pricing Creativity: A Guide to Profit Beyond the Billable Hour* (2018).**
The modern bible for creative/expert firms. Three pillars:
1. **Specialize deeply** → become irreplaceable.
2. **Diagnostic conversations, not pitches** → you sell by *helping them see the problem*, not by listing capabilities.
3. **Value-based pricing** → never hourly. Price the *outcome*, not the *effort*.

The framework maps perfectly onto DS2's **challenge-first** method — challenge *is* the diagnostic conversation. The "decision pause" is the value-based-pricing setup ("we don't need an answer today"). You're already 80% Win-Without-Pitching native; you just need to name it and price like it.

**Alan Weiss — *Million Dollar Consulting* (1992, multiple eds), *The Consulting Bible* (2011).**
Solo-and-small-firm tactics. The mantra: **price outcomes, not deliverables**. Weiss's "fee table" worksheet alone will reset how you scope a project.

### Tier 2 — pricing & sales mechanics

**Jonathan Stark — *Hourly Billing Is Nuts* / "Ditching Hourly" podcast.**
Hourly billing punishes efficiency, anchors you cheap, and caps your revenue at hours-in-the-day. Stark's value-pricing onboarding (called *value conversations*) is bite-sized and immediately applicable.

**Philip Morgan — *The Positioning Manual for Technical Firms* (2017).**
Specifically for technical consultancies (DS2's bucket). Positions through a **horizontal** (a problem) and/or **vertical** (an industry) axis. Cheap, short, useful exercises.

**Mahan Khalsa — *Let's Get Real or Let's Not Play* (1999, FranklinCovey reissue 2008).**
Diagnostic selling. The opposite of pitching. Helping the client reach the right answer (even if it means walking away) — *which is exactly what DS2 already does in the four beats of challenge-first*.

### Tier 3 — distribution & psychology

**Robert Cialdini — *Influence* (1984), *Pre-Suasion* (2016).**
The six principles (reciprocity, commitment, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity) — but read it for the *honest application to expert services*, not the dark-pattern interpretation.

**Bob Burg — *Endless Referrals* (1994).**
Systematic referral generation. Referrals are the cheapest distribution channel for a consultancy; treat the system like a discipline.

**Mark Schaefer — *Marketing Rebellion* (2019); *Belonging to the Brand* (2023).**
Modern POV on B2B distribution in a world where ads work less and community/POV works more.

## The actual money equation (unpacked)

### 1. WEDGE — be famous for ONE thing in ONE market
The strongest finding across every source above: **niche down or stay invisible**. The biggest reason small consultancies plateau is they keep saying yes to off-wedge work. Boutique firms that doubled revenue almost always *cut* services first (see Visible Authority's 30%-cut case).

For DS2 → see [03-wedge-and-positioning.md](03-wedge-and-positioning.md). Pick one wedge for the next 18 months. Re-evaluate then.

### 2. NAMED METHOD — productize your way of working
DS2 already has the rare thing: a *real* method (Challenge-First Delivery, four beats). Most consultancies have values, not methods. Productize it:
- **Name it** ("Challenge-First Delivery™" — yes, the ™ matters psychologically).
- **One-page diagram** of the four beats.
- **A book / long essay** that *is* the canonical reference (Lean Startup did this for "build-measure-learn"; Lochhead did it for "category design").
- **A talk** you give 4× a year, in the wedge industry's conferences.
- **A workshop** — sellable as a 1-day engagement to pre-clients.

### 3. PROOF — make every engagement a case study
- A written case study (problem → diagnosis → challenge → alternative chosen → outcome) for *every* engagement.
- Permission to name the client built into the engagement contract.
- Public artifacts: blog post, podcast appearance, conference talk per engagement.
- Pro-bono engagements early on are *worth it for the proof* — but only if you can talk about them.

### 4. DISTRIBUTION — pick one cadence and never miss
Boutique consultancies that grow inbound do **one** of these religiously, not many sporadically:
- **POV essay/month** — long-form, your own site, syndicated to LinkedIn.
- **LinkedIn**: 2 posts/week, opinionated, not promotional. (Both founders. Use voice.)
- **Podcast appearances**: 6–12/year on niche industry podcasts.
- **Talks**: 2–4 conferences/year in the wedge industry.
- **Email list**: weekly note. Compounds slower but is owned distribution.
- **Referral system**: Burg's playbook, formalized.
The mistake is doing 5 of these badly. Pick one. Do it well.

### 5. PREMIUM PRICING — never hourly, always banded
- Engagement bands (e.g., €25k diagnostic / €120k build / €15k/mo Stewardship).
- Outcomes language ("we close the gap between funded and shipped"), not deliverables language.
- Stewardship as a productized monthly retainer (the start of recurring revenue).
- Walk away from off-wedge work, even when revenue is needed (especially then). The discipline is the asset.

### 6. SENIOR-ONLY DELIVERY — DS2's hidden advantage
Most consultancies sell with seniors and deliver with juniors. DS2 delivers with seniors throughout — make this **explicit on every touchpoint**:
- Site copy: "You'll always work directly with a founder."
- Sales calls: introduce the people who'll do the work, not BD reps.
- Pricing rationale: this is *why* you cost more.

## Tactical playbook for the next 90 days (post-wedge decision)

1. Write the **manifesto essay** (~2,000 words) on Challenge-First Delivery. It's the cornerstone POV.
2. Build the **one-page diagram** of the four beats. Use it on the site + sales deck + LinkedIn.
3. Publish **1 case study** (real or pro-bono).
4. Set up the **monthly POV cadence** — calendar-block writing time.
5. Define the **engagement bands** + **Stewardship monthly retainer** price. Put on the site or hold in the back pocket — at minimum, the founders know them cold.
6. Apply to **2 niche conferences** in the wedge industry.

That's a quarter of work — but each piece is reusable indefinitely. This is the asset stack that compounds.

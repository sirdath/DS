## 1. What we are

**DS is a Digital Solutions Consultancy.**

Category: **Digital Solutions Consultancy** (also acceptable: *digital solutions consultancy / agency*). We deliberately do **not** say "tech consulting" alone or "web dev agency" alone, the name describes the outcome, not the method, so we can do consulting, delivery, or both without repositioning.

## 2. Where we are

**Athens–London based.** We operate between Athens and Greece-based clients and London and UK-based clients. Website footer and intros use one of:

- "Athens–London based digital solutions consultancy" *(default)*
- "Operating between Athens and London" *(safe alternative)*
- "Headquartered in Athens, with a London presence" *(most conservative)*

Rule: we only claim London presence as long as there is real, ongoing activity there (founder/director/team member based there). Don't imply offices we don't have.

## 3. Who leads

Two-head structure, used externally on the site, in pitches, and on LinkedIn:

| Title (external) | Owns | Optional internal/legal title |
|---|---|---|
| **Head of Strategy & Consulting** | Client relationships, business problems, commercial strategy, marketing, consulting engagements | Managing Director |
| **Head of Engineering & Data** | Architecture, engineering quality, data / ML decisions, technical delivery, tech stack | Technical Director |

One-sentence structure explanation:
> *"The firm is led by a Head of Strategy & Consulting and a Head of Engineering & Data, covering both business advisory and technical delivery."*

Do **not** use: CEO/CTO (overkill at this size), "Lead Developer" + "Business Lead" (too junior), "Innovation Officer" / "Digital Guru" (buzzwordy).

## 4. How we work, Challenge-first, Transparent Delivery

This is the differentiator. It's not "a value", it's a **structured, repeatable workstyle**.

### Four-beat engagement pattern
1. **Diagnose & challenge**, we explicitly assess what's working, what isn't, and what we'd do differently, even if it contradicts the original brief.
2. **Say what creates risk**, never "this is wrong"; always "this creates risk because…", "this may not scale due to…", "from experience this usually leads to…". Protective framing, not judgmental.
3. **Present alternatives with reasoning**, critique is always paired with a constructive alternative that explains trade-offs, what changes, and what it unlocks.
4. **Decision pause**, explicit: *"You don't need to decide now. Take time, we're happy to proceed either way."* This is rare, powerful, memorable.

### Signature sentences (memorize these)
- *"We work best when we can be honest early, even if that means challenging the initial idea. We'll always explain our thinking and leave the decision with you."* (first-meeting opener)
- *"Projects end; responsibility doesn't."* (closing line on monthly Stewardship)
- *"We don't certify your organisation, we take responsibility for what we build."* (product assurance)

## 5. The 9-step delivery process

| # | Step | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Context & External Analysis | Business model, market, users, constraints, understand *why* the problem exists |
| 2 | Technical & Digital Assessment | Current state of the client's site/app/stack, the honesty checkpoint |
| 3 | Challenge & Alignment Discussion | Two-way conversation on findings; pause if we can't align |
| 4 | Internal Deep-dive & Synthesis | Connect business goals + technical realities + user needs + constraints |
| 5 | Proposed Solution & Options | Recommended direction + alternatives + trade-offs + mockups/diagrams |
| 6 | Decision Pause | Client reviews, asks hard questions, decides without pressure |
| 7 | Side-by-side Development & Iteration | Visible delivery with checkpoints, demos, early flagging of issues |
| 8 | Implementation, Launch & Handover | Deploy, documentation, training, knowledge transfer |
| 9 | Review & Learning | Reflect on what worked, what didn't, what we'd do differently |

### Phase 2, Stewardship (optional, opt-in monthly)
After delivery, clients can opt into an ongoing monthly engagement. This is **not** "filled hours", it's continuity, technical care, and advisory.

- What's included: maintenance, monitoring, small improvements, advisory, early warning
- What's **not** included: big new builds (those are separate scope)
- Rules for the team: no work without a reason; continuous challenge continues; regular clarity checkpoints (monthly or quarterly); clients free to start, pause, or stop at any time

## 6. Engagement modes

Clients engage us in exactly one of three ways per project:

1. **Consulting-only**, advisory, discovery, architecture, vendor selection. Solution delivered as insight. We state independence explicitly: *"Our consulting can be completely standalone, some clients implement internally or with other vendors."*
2. **Build-only**, website, app, MVP, redesign from spec. Solution *is* the thing.
3. **End-to-end**, discovery → design → development → launch. One accountable partner.

Plus (optionally added to 2 or 3): **Stewardship** (the monthly mode above).

Website must surface all three clearly, see `docs/brand/SITEMAP.md` (to be written).

## 7. Product assurance (not enterprise security)

**Our position:** *"Built to hold up under stress"*. We stress-test and apply risk controls to the **products and platforms we deliver**. We do **not** audit organisations, legacy systems, or infrastructure-wide risk.

### What we do test in what we ship
- Load and performance behaviour
- Failure scenarios and edge cases
- Data handling and access boundaries
- Degradation paths, what breaks first and how
- Dependencies and third-party exposure inside the product

### What we explicitly don't
> *Risk controls apply to the solution we deliver. Organisational, legal, or infrastructure-wide risks sit outside this scope.*

Placement on the site: a module on relevant Services pages + a subsection on "How we work" under delivery ("We test what we ship"). **Never** a standalone "Security" page, that creates liability we haven't signed up for.

## 8. Tone of voice

- **Candid but collaborative.** Use "you/we". Direct, not performative.
- **Truth-teller, not insult-teller.** *"This creates risk because…"*, never *"this is wrong"*.
- **Sentence-case headlines**, easier to scan, more modern than Title Case.
- **Short, rhythmic sentences.** Consulting-grade clarity, not marketing fluff.
- **Avoid:** "innovation", "synergy", "transformation" (unqualified), "guru", "ninja", "hybrid innovation studio", "tech-powered consultancy collective". These read as fluff to procurement.

## 9. Website structure (locked from DS2)

### Top nav (5–6 items)
Home · Services · Work · How we work · Insights *(optional)* · **Contact** *(button style)*

### Homepage scroll story
1. **Hero**, one-line value prop + honesty subhead + primary CTA ("Book a call" / "Request a review") + secondary CTA ("View work")
2. **What we do**, 3–5 capability cards (Strategy & discovery · Web/app development · Data & ML if core · Delivery & implementation · Ongoing support)
3. **What makes us different**, institutionalize the honesty model (Challenge early · Show risks + alternatives · You decide · No surprises)
4. **Process preview**, 5–7-step snapshot of the 9-step process
5. **Proof**, 2–4 case studies in *Problem → Approach → Outcome* format
6. **Trust signals**, testimonials / logos (only if permitted) / "ways we work" / certifications
7. **Engage us**, three pathways explicit (Consulting-only / Build-only / End-to-end)
8. **Final CTA**, "Tell us what you're building" / "Request an assessment"

### Footer
About · Careers *(optional)* · Privacy / Terms · Socials · **Locations, Athens · London**

## 10. Case study (Work page) template

Every case study follows the same structure. Especially for rebuild/redesign work, use the **Transformation Story** format, never a naked before/after slider.

### Sections per case study
1. **Snapshot**, industry, timeline, what you delivered
2. **Challenge**, the problem in the client's language
3. **What we changed**, decisions, not features. Bullet list of *decisions* (restructured IA around user intent, reduced page weight, introduced component system, etc.)
4. **Solution**, visuals + architecture where relevant
5. **Outcome**, metrics if we have them, capability outcomes otherwise (maintainability, clarity, control)
6. **CTA**, *"Want a similar result?"*

### Before/After rules
- **Same viewport, same crop.** That's what makes it credible.
- **Desktop:** side-by-side (Before left, greyscale / slightly faded; After right, full colour) with a caption explaining *why* the change matters.
- **Mobile:** toggle switch, same viewport, static text below.
- **Label the section:** "Transformations" / "Rebuilds" / "Design & platform evolution". **Never** "Before & After" alone.

## 11. Services / How we work, page structure

### Service page template
Header → Feature (why it matters now) → Capabilities (concrete list, no fluff) → Outcomes (measurable value) → CTA → Credentials + related work → Enquiry form

### "How we work" page additions
- **How we communicate**, weekly update rhythm, decision logs, risks flagged early
- **Decision points**, after assessment (go/no-go) → after mockups (scope lock) → after MVP (launch readiness)
